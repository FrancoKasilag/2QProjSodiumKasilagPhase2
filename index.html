Inhuman Reflexes: A Look At Modern Competitive Tetris

Tetris (1984)

I think we're all familiar with the concept and basic rules of Tetris
(drop pieces, form horizontal lines, don't reach the top) so I'll skip over that,
but a lot of younger people now don't actually know that Tetris started with
Nintendo's NES Tetris from 1984. Back when NES Tetris was pretty much
the only version of Tetris played, there wasn't really a "competitive" scene,
since at the time Social Media didn't exist, the concept of Esports wasn't a
thing, and most kids only played with their friends for fun. This is a large
reason why Tetris even now isn't really seen as a spectator sport, but
hidden under the old 1984 facade is a GIANT scene of competition.

After a while, Tetris got imported from the NES game console to the PC
and got several versions and remakes, making slight gameplay mechanic
changes (more on this in the linked pages). Hardware has evolved
massively as well, with the rise of popularity of mechanical keyboards,
gaming monitors and very powerful computers. To add, the abilities of
players have improved immensely, i.e. reaction time, finger speed,
hand-eye coordination, and evolution of strategies. Because of all this,
pro Tetris players from both the 1984 Classic Tetris game and the
modern PC ports of Tetris got INSANELY GOOD.
